Inglewood Sellers Face a Longer Launch Window in July 2026

Inglewood housing market update for July 2026: Longer market time makes the first two weeks, showing access, and price evidence more important.

By Israel Hernandez Updated July 7, 2026 Los Angeles County July 2026 report Latest MLS period: June 2026
More choice means your launch has to prove its price. Inglewood is showing 83 active listings, 5.0 months of supply, and 23 median days on market. The year-over-year read: inventory down 4.6% year over year, pending sales up 100.0% year over year, and median sale price down 8.7% year over year. Buyers have more alternatives, so the sale plan should make price, condition, access, and showing strategy feel obvious before the listing starts collecting days.

The narrative behind the numbers

Active inventory is 83 in June 2026, down 4.6% from a year earlier. That is the public competition set your launch has to sit inside, especially if buyers are also comparing Inglewood with Hawthorne and El Segundo.

Demand needs a leading and lagging read. Pending sales are 14, and closed sales are 13. Pending activity shows what buyers are choosing now; closed sales prove what already happened. Price, prep, access, and showing strategy should respond to both.

Pricing needs more than a headline number. The median sale price is $790,000, while average price per square foot is $627. Those are useful anchors, but Inglewood can shift quickly by stadium and airport-adjacent context, freeway access, parking, older systems, zoning context, and commute needs. Because product mix can change quickly, read each signal against the actual homes competing with yours before choosing price or prep.

The decision in front of you

If current demand is keeping up with inventory, a clean, well-priced launch can still earn attention. If inventory is rising, days are stretching, or buyer payment pressure is showing up, the listing needs sharper price proof, cleaner prep, stronger access, and fewer unanswered objections.

Best next step:

Before listing, compare your home against active alternatives, recent pendings, the last 30-60 days of closed sales, condition gaps, buyer objections, required reports or permit questions, and your real timing and net-proceeds goals.

Advisor note

Inglewood is a place where stadium and airport-adjacent context, freeway access, parking, older systems, zoning context, and commute needs can change the entire launch read. I would use the market data as a pressure test, then set the pricing, prep, access, and timing plan property by property.

The safest posture is neither panic nor overconfidence. Know what the current numbers say, know what your home can prove, and remove the objections buyers are most likely to use against the price.

FAQ

Is now a good time to sell in Inglewood?

Use the market as a pricing and timing check, not a yes-or-no answer. Active inventory is 83, pending sales are 14, and months supply is 5.0. Read those with condition, access, showing activity, and nearby competition before choosing a launch plan.

Can I price aggressively in Inglewood right now?

Only if the home can defend the number. Median sale price is $790,000, average price per square foot is $627, and median days active in MLS is 23. Those are starting points; the list price still needs recent comparable sales, condition proof, and a clean buyer-confidence story.

Are closed sales hiding a softer current Inglewood market?

They can. Closed sales are 13, but closings usually reflect decisions made weeks earlier. Compare them with pending sales, active inventory, new listings, and days on market before relying on closed-sale strength alone.

What should I fix or document before listing?

Start with the items most likely to create buyer hesitation: condition, access, permits, required local reports, HOA documents if applicable, insurance questions, disclosure issues, and anything affected by stadium and airport-adjacent context, freeway access, parking, older systems, zoning context, and commute needs.

What should I watch before choosing a list price?

Watch active competition, recent pendings, the last 30-60 days of closed sales, days on market, showing activity, buyer objections, price reductions where available, and the gap between your timing needs and the market's current pace.
